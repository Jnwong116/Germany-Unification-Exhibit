September 1866: To Use a Needle

This caricature is from a French journal, but is included in this exhibit because it gives some insight into how the rest of the world, particularly France, who would shortly face Prussia’s army, reacted to the war between Prussia and Austria.
In the caricature, there is a Prussian soldier who tries to stitch together the defeated states into Prussia. The cloth has Frankfurt, Hanover, Saxony, and Nassau written on the right hand side and Prussia written on the left. The caption says: “It is one thing to know how to use a needle… But it’s a skill that should not be abused”. The needle is a nice reference to the needle gun the Prussians used to defeat the Austrian forces.
This caricature shows the anti-Prussian sentiment in France and gives us a picture of how the rest of Europe would have received this news. The French clearly see Prussia as an abusive power who is poorly attempting to unify the German states, as seen by the German soldier’s stitching job.
